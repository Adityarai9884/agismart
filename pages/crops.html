FAQs

Common Crop Questions

Conduct a soil test to determine texture, pH (ideal 6–7 for most crops) and nutrient levels. Sandy soils suit groundnut and potato; clay soils are ideal for rice; loam soils work for most cereals and vegetables. Match the crop's water requirement to your irrigation capacity.
Crop rotation means growing different crops in the same field in successive seasons. It breaks pest and disease cycles, improves soil structure, replenishes nutrients (especially when legumes are included), and reduces dependency on chemical fertilisers.
For wheat: apply half the nitrogen + full phosphorus + full potassium as basal dose at sowing. Apply the remaining nitrogen in two splits — first at crown root initiation (21 days) and second at jointing stage (45 days). Always follow the soil test-based recommendation for your area.
Use Integrated Pest Management (IPM): install pheromone traps, use neem-based biopesticides, practice intercropping to confuse pests, encourage natural predators like ladybirds and spiders, and follow proper field sanitation. Chemical pesticides should be the last resort.
